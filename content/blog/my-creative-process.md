Every project starts with a conversation. Whether it's a brand commercial, a music video, or a 3D product showcase, the process follows a similar arc.

## 1. Understanding the Brief

I start by asking questions:

- Who is the audience?
- What emotion should the piece evoke?
- What are the technical constraints (duration, format, delivery)?
- What references or inspirations does the client have?

## 2. Research & References

I collect visual references, create mood boards, and study similar work. This isn't about copying — it's about understanding the visual language of the genre.

## 3. Rough Cut / Draft

For video projects, I assemble a rough cut quickly. The goal at this stage is structure and pacing, not polish. For motion graphics, I sketch keyframes and blocking.

## 4. Iteration

This is where the magic happens. I refine timing, adjust color, layer in sound design, and go back and forth with the client. Most projects go through 2-3 revision cycles.

## 5. Polish & Deliver

Final color grade, audio mix, and export settings tailored to the delivery platform. I always watch the final export all the way through before sending.

Every project teaches me something new. That's what keeps this work exciting.
